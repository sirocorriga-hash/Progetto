One team, one region

Cagliari Calcio is the pride of an entire region, one of the most beloved teams in Italy. The 1970 Scudetto represented its pinnacle, but the Rossoblu's history is read like a life story. The good and the bad, the joy and the pain: exhilarating victories, unforgettable goals, but also bitter defeats and disastrous setbacks, from which Cagliari has always recovered. Stories of love and pride, of hard work and sacrifice. The Rossoblu epic awaits the next chapter.

Champions 1970

Cagliari's Scudetto represented Sardinia's true entry into Italy. It was the event that sealed Sardinia's definitive entry into the history of Italian culture. Sardinia needed a major breakthrough, and it achieved it through football, beating the mighty Milan and Turin, traditionally the capitals of Italian football. The Scudetto allowed Sardinia to free itself from long-standing inferiority complexes, and it was a positive achievement, a joyful event.

The season 25/26

Having parted ways with Davide Nicola, who had led Cagliari to safety the previous season, the Sardinian team hired new coach Fabio Pisacane directly from its youth team.The summer transfer window saw Cagliari intent on strengthening several areas, such as the defense, where Zé Pedro and Juan Rodríguez arrived permanently from Porto and Peñarol respectively, along with the loan arrival of Marco Palestra from Atalanta. In midfield, the loan arrivals of Michael Folorunsho from Napoli and Luca Mazzitelli from Como were finalized, in addition to the redemption of Michael Adopo from Atalanta.

Sardinia: an island that is almost a continent

Slow steps, curious and inspiring eyes, a serene soul yearning for adventure: this is how you walk through the small villages and pristine nature of Sardinia, immersed in an atmosphere of purity and relaxation, far from the crowds and bustle. It is the intimate and authentic face of a mythical land, where you can clearly perceive the traces, both material and otherwise, left by historical events and men of faith who, centuries or millennia later, continue to inspire the intense devotion of hikers, pilgrims, and visitors to those places.
