Cagliari Football Club: one team, one region

Cagliari Calcio is the pride of an entire region, one of the most beloved teams in Italy. The 1970 Scudetto represented its pinnacle, but the Rossoblu's history is read like a life story. The good and the bad, the joy and the pain: exhilarating victories, unforgettable goals, but also bitter defeats and disastrous setbacks, from which Cagliari has always recovered. Stories of love and pride, of hard work and sacrifice. The Rossoblu epic awaits the next chapter.

Thunderclap: our man

The story of Cagliari's greatest moments is the story of its greatest player, Gigi Riva. The Sardinian striker was the symbol of the 1970 Scudetto, and his name is forever linked to that triumph. Riva's goals were decisive, his presence on the field was inspiring, and his love for Cagliari was evident in every match. He was not just a player; he was a legend, a hero for the fans, and an icon of Sardinian football. His legacy continues to inspire new generations of players and fans alike.

The season 1969/70

Having parted ways with Davide Nicola, who had led Cagliari to safety the previous season, the Sardinian team hired new coach Fabio Pisacane directly from its youth team.The summer transfer window saw Cagliari intent on strengthening several areas, such as the defense, where Zé Pedro and Juan Rodríguez arrived permanently from Porto and Peñarol respectively, along with the loan arrival of Marco Palestra from Atalanta. In midfield, the loan arrivals of Michael Folorunsho from Napoli and Luca Mazzitelli from Como were finalized, in addition to the redemption of Michael Adopo from Atalanta.
